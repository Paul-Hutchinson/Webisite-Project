There are several risk factors when carrying out any digital project that should always be taken in to account to help mitigate the risk of failure. The main risk from an outside source is cyber security and risks of data breach/loss, as well as hijacking. This has the potential to not only jeopardise the project, but also the entire company. Other risks that could lead to project failure is mainly hardware related issue, which may not always be within your reach of fixing. Typically an accident or malfunction could cause hard drive corruption, or even just specific files could become corrupted. If processes such as backups locally, and off site (via cloud or physically) have not been carried out, these files may be lost forever. If internet goes down in your area, this may also lead to delivery dates being pushed/missed.

1.7


The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) has an impact on any digital platform and interacts/stores its users data, as well as email/contact lists. GDPR was put introduce by the EU on May 25th, 2018, and enforces these regulations using large fines if violated. GDPR was introduced by the EU to “harmonise” data privacy laws across all members of the European Union. It also allows individuals the right to request how their personal data is being collected and stored from individual companies. The large fines imposed if GDPR is breached is extremely important to keep in mind when starting any digital project that may require users data.
